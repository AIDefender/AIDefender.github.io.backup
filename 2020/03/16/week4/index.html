Equivalence Between PG and SQL

It is shown that there is a precise equivalence between Q-learning and policy gradient methods in the setting of entropy-regularized reinforcement learning. To be accurate, the gradient of the loss function used in n-step Q-learning is equal to the gradient of the loss used in an n-step policy gradient method, including a squared-error term on the value function.

Emphatic TD Learning  TD learning: make online adaptations to the prediction based on one(or several) steps, rather than the whole trajectory. Using IS to reweight the updates of TD in off-policy setting has very high variance. Emphatic weighting is similar to IS in that it also varies emphasis so as to reweight the distribution of linear TD($λ$) updates, but to a different goal. The new goal is to create a weighting equivalent to the followon distribution for the target policy started in the stationary distribution of the behavior policy.

  • stability of on-policy TD(0): many matrix-form annotations; related to positive definite matrix and contraction.
  • instability of off-policy TD(0): distribution mismatch and the $\theta\to2\theta$ problem.

Details of Off-policy TD  The excursion objective is

Basic assumption in off-TD is that $\mu(a|s)>0$ for every $s,a$ for which $\pi(a|s)>0$, since it would be impossible to estimate the values under $\pi$ if the actions that $\pi$ would take were never taken by $\mu$ and their consequences were never observed. This is called the assumption of coverage.

The deep reason for the difficulty of off-policy learning is that the behavior policy may take the process to a distribution of states different from that which would be encountered under the target policy, yet the states might appear to be the same or similar because of function approximation.
